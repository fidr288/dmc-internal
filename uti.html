Urinary tract infection in females


Standing order

Indication

Females >16 who has symptoms of UTI (dysuria, frequency and positive dipstick for leucocytes) but NO fever or loin pain

OR

Female >16 with lab result confirming UTI sensitive to nitrofurantoin.

Dosage

Nitrofurantoin 50mg 4 times daily , for 5 days. (7 days for pregnant women)

NOT FOR PREGNANT WOMEN > 36 WEEKS

Contraindications

  • Pregnancy > 36 weeks
  • Allergy to nitrofurantoin
  • eGFR < 45mL/min

Precautions

  • Renal impariment
  • Severe liver disease

Side effects

Common: anorexia, nausea, vomiting , diarrhoea

Severe: cholestatic jaundice, hepatitis, erythema multiforme, pancreatitis, arthralgia.

Documentation

Notes must include:

  • Any allergies or not
  • Details of person administering
  • Medication given, route, dose, duration and expiry
  • Record the absence of vomiting, high fevers/ rigors, loin pain, vaginal discharge.
  • Patients with recurrent UTI (>2 episodes in past 6 months) unless previously investigated , diabetes, spinal injuries, spina bifida, indwelling catheters , children â€“ refer to the doctor.

Information

  • All patients given nitrofurantoin should have advice on medication and side effects.
  • Patients who have been sexually active should also have STI screening done as STIs can present with similar symptoms.
  • Return if not improved after 48 hours.
  • Stop medication and return if a severe side effects
